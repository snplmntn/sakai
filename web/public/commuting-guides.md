# Commuting Guide (Philippines)

This guide gives practical, beginner-friendly steps for common commute modes in Metro Manila.

## Walking
Walking is unavoidable in many trips. Stay alert and prioritize safety at all times.

### Tips for Walking
- Stay on sidewalks whenever possible and avoid walking on active roads.
- Keep your bag zipped and your belongings secure.
- Stay aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
- Look toward oncoming traffic before crossing.
- Cross at a steady pace and avoid stopping in the middle of the road.
- Do not text or chat while crossing.

## Jeepney
Jeepneys are one of the most common public transport modes. They usually follow fixed routes and are used for both short and long trips.

### How to Ride a Jeepney
1. Check the signboard on the windshield or side to confirm the route.
2. Raise your hand to hail a jeepney.
3. If unsure, ask the driver if it passes your destination before boarding.
4. After sitting, pay by saying "Bayad po" and pass your fare.
5. When near your stop, say "Para po" to signal the driver.
6. Keep your belongings close and secure while riding.

## MRT/LRT
MRT and LRT lines are good for longer metro trips and direct station-to-station travel. Trains can be crowded during rush hour.

### How to Ride MRT/LRT
1. Queue at the ticket booth or vending machine.
2. Check the fare chart for your destination station.
3. Choose either:
   - Single-journey card
   - Beep stored-value card
4. For single-journey cards, tell the cashier your destination and pay.
5. For Beep cards, purchase a card, then load/reload balance as needed.
6. At the gate:
   - Insert single-journey cards in the slot.
   - Tap Beep cards on the scanner.
7. Queue properly and let passengers alight before boarding.
8. Listen for station announcements and prepare before your stop.
9. Exit by inserting (single-journey) or tapping (Beep) at the gate.
10. Follow station signs, or ask security personnel if needed.

## Bus
Buses are useful for longer distances, including city and provincial routes.

### How to Ride the Bus
1. Check the front signboard to verify the route.
2. Raise your hand to hail the bus.
3. Board carefully and find a seat if available.
4. Pay the conductor and clearly state your destination.
5. Keep your ticket for inspection.
6. Listen for stop announcements and move toward the door in advance.

## Tricycle
Tricycles are typically used for short-distance travel.

### How to Ride a Tricycle
1. Clarify if the trip is regular/shared or special/private before boarding.
2. For special trips, tell the driver your destination first.
3. For regular trips, signal "Para po" near your destination.
4. Pay after alighting and confirm the fare politely.

## FX (UV Express)
FX/UV Express rides are usually more comfortable than jeepneys and commonly operate terminal-based routes.

### How to Ride FX / UV Express
1. Check the route signboard at the front or side.
2. Hail the vehicle or board at a terminal queue.
3. Pay by saying "Bayad po" and state your destination.
4. If seated far from the driver, ask co-passengers to pass your fare.
5. Say "Para po" when near your stop.

## Taxi
Taxis can bring you directly to your destination. Fare is meter-based.

### How to Ride a Taxi
1. Confirm with the driver that your destination is accepted before boarding.
2. Ensure the meter is running.
3. Share route guidance if the driver is unfamiliar with your destination.
4. Pay only upon arrival and ask for a receipt when available.

### Taxi Safety Tips
- Note the taxi plate number and send it to a trusted contact.
- Stay alert during the trip.
- If anything feels unsafe, ask to be dropped off in a safe public area.

## Comet E-Jeep
Comet E-Jeep rides are similar to regular jeepneys but use electric vehicles and a card-based payment flow.

### How to Ride the Comet E-Jeep
1. Buy a reloadable card at the terminal booth.
2. Load or reload balance before boarding.
3. Tap your card at the vehicle sensor upon entry.
4. Alight only at designated stops along the route.

## Quick Commute Safety Checklist
- Keep valuables secure and out of sight.
- Avoid using your phone while crossing roads.
- Prepare small bills or exact fare when possible.
- Verify destination signs before boarding.
- When unsure, ask drivers, conductors, or station staff.
